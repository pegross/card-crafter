The Long Quiet

Every warm, bright, hopeful thing you do to stay alive — most of all the light you keep in the window for someone who may never come — draws the dead a little closer.

Genre  Survival card-sim, real-time-with-pause Engine  Godot 4.7 · GDScript Platform  PC / desktop Scope  Learning vertical slice Art  Text-forward / iconographic Deck?  None — cards are objects on a table
§ 01

Elevator Pitch

The Long Quiet is a text-forward, real-time-with-pause survival card game in the lineage of Card Survival: Tropical Island — no deck, no hand, just cards-as-objects on a table you drag together to boil water, feed the fire, dress wounds, and eat before the food rots. There is no health bar and there is no enemy you fight.

The zombies are weather. The indifferent dead never hunt you; they drift down a Presence gradient toward the loudest, brightest, warmest thing in a frozen valley. And that thing is you — because every act that keeps you alive (light a fire, cook a meal, chop wood, bleed) docks a trailing signal card — HeatLightNoiseScent — that sums into one Presence value shown before you commit. Survival and safety are the same dial pulled in opposite directions, so there are no dead chores.

Each dusk you face the game's aching choice: split your last fuel between the hearth that keeps your body alive and the lamp you light in the window for Maren, last known alive across the valley — hope, warmth's rival, and the brightest beacon you own. A visible, fragile ladder called The Road inches forward each morning that followed a lit night, so hope is something you watch climb, not an invisible coin flip.

You do not beat this game. You author the specific, mundane, dignified way one winter week ends, logged day by day into a persistent obituary journal.

§ 02

Design Pillars

Four load-bearing ideas. Every feature must sharpen one of them or it is cut.

Presence: the beacon you are

One unifying signal fuses safety and survival into a single dial. Heat, Light, Noise and Scent are literal trailing cards docked to your Survivor that sum into one value on a pip strip H2L4N0S2Σ8. The dead drift down that gradient rather than targeting you. Every efficient action is also a broadcast — each drag's Presence delta is shown before you commit, and every loud daytime action previews the night it will author ("Chop: +8 Noise now · tonight's approach reaches the Door ~01:40"). The one act that overrides caution is fighting: the panic axe-swing is the only action in the game with no preview.

Interlocking attrition, not health bars

The real game is the slow, compounding neglect of parallel needs — two-layer hunger, thirst, Warmth, energy, and a single infection counter — each with staged effects, surfaced through a pinned, auto-sorted danger list. Illness is never rolled; it self-assembles from your own status cards using the same drag-to-combine grammar that crafts a tool (Wet + Cold + Exhausted → Fever). Death arrives as a traceable cascade you can narrate card by card — Wet → Cold → Fever → idle → freeze — never a single bar hitting zero.

The Vigil: hope you can watch climb

You keep a lamp lit in the window for Maren, last known alive across the valley. Lighting it slows your Solitude decay, feeds Hope, and advances The Road — a visible ladder of named rungs ("footprints at the treeline" → "smoke on the ridge, closer" → "a figure on the road"), the one thing in the week that grows instead of drains. But the lamp is the brightest signal you own, and an unlit night slips The Road back a rung. Never lighting it isn't free either: within a few dark nights the Depressed spiral accelerates hunger and thirst and stalls your hands.

Melancholic legibility

Every drag lists its time, yield, requirement and Presence delta up front, so physical deaths feel authored, not arbitrary — which is exactly what invites one more run. But legibility is applied with a scalpel: the mind is felt, not itemized (Solitude surfaces as a single band word — "The quiet is unbearable" — never a float), P(arrival) is qualitative bands only, and the bite stays face-down. Flat, clinical, Project Zomboid prose; empty days are logged; each run ends in a dated obituary card in a journal that persists across runs.

The core hook, in one line You must be warm, fed, and busy to survive — yet every warm, fed, busy act makes you the loudest thing in a silent world. You never beat a zombie; you spend the whole game trying to be a smaller sound. And the single loudest thing you can choose to be is hopeful.
§ 03

The Core Loop

Wake, secure needs quietly, make a timed daytime run, maintain the pipelines, advance The Road, then face the dusk fuel-split and endure a telegraphed night. Sleep, log the day, repeat toward the Day-7 resolution.

  1. Wake. Read the auto-sorted danger list (most-lethal need pinned top) and the Presence strip on your Survivor — how warm, bright, loud and bloody are you, and what does today's accumulated exposure forecast for tonight's approach?

  2. Secure acute needs quietly, each with cost / yield / Presence shown before you drag: drink stored Boiled Water; eat against two-layer hunger (cold = silent, no warmth; cooked = warm and higher morale but mints Scent); feed the fire (Heat + Light + Noise).

  3. Travel. Choose a timed run from the Cabin hub to the Woodline (20m) or Creek (15m). The world keeps ticking — the hearth burns down at home, cold bites you the whole walk. Chop firewood or fill Dirty Water; triage into 6 inventory slots; loose Ground cards left behind are abandoned.

  4. Maintain the pipelines. Boil a full pot of water at once and store it, keep the hearth fed, eat foraged food before it spoils, dress a wound before it turns dirty — and read the two Found Notes (one in Maren's hand) for a small cache or a better-boil recipe.

  5. Advance the long horizon. Tune the degrading radio at dusk (a shrinking morale lift, one of 7 serialized messages dying toward static), dwell on a keepsake to blunt Solitude, and watch The Road for the rung a lit night would win — or a dark night would lose.

  6. Face the dusk decision. The projection shows both stakes side by side: lamp (Hope+, Solitude slowed, The Road advances — but tonight's peak Presence spikes and they will come) vs. dark (safe — but Solitude accelerates toward Depressed and The Road slips back). Split finite fuel between hearth and vigil lamp.

  7. Endure the night. A high-Presence night converges the Dead across four telegraphed states — Treeline → Yard → Door → Breach — over hours, never a jump scare. Go dark (dowse the fire, beginning a cold cascade whose cost grows as the week deepens), hold still, or accept the breach's auto-Bite. Then sleep, log Day N and the weather, and weigh warmth vs. quiet vs. hope again.

§ 04

Key Twists

What makes this stand apart from both Card Survival and every other zombie game.

Presence-as-beacon

Efficient survival is loud survival. Heat, Noise, Scent and Light are trailing cards summing to one detectability value; "the horde" is a systemic pressure gradient you manage by being smaller, not an encounter you fight. Every loud daytime action previews its contribution to tonight's approach — closing the gap between cause (chopping at noon) and consequence (the shape crossing the yard at 1am).

The vigil lamp & The Road

Each night you can spend scarce fuel to light a lamp for Maren. It is the brightest signal you own — and it advances The Road, a visible ladder of named rungs in a flat, unreliable-narrator voice. Hope is something you watch inch forward, not a hidden percentage. The Day-7 payoff is gated off the visible rung reached — a legible culmination, never a coin flip.

Both keepsakes are fuel

Maren's mended sweater and one dull photograph each carry a real Warmth value alongside a Hope value that fades each time you dwell (grief wears the photo smooth). On the Day-6 Deep-Freeze night, holding Warmth on firewood alone may be impossible, and the game presents the sentiment-vs-survival gut-punch as a real choice of which to burn — a permanent Grief status and Hope-ceiling loss. Emergent pressure, never a hard "burn-or-die" gate.

Illness self-assembles

No disease is rolled. Fever crystallizes when Wet + Cold + Exhausted coexist on your Survivor for 30 continuous minutes; Infection begins when a Dirty Wound meets the Malnourished flag. Same drag-to-combine grammar as crafting, evaluated passively each tick. Each result card prints its own recipe and break condition, so you fight the conditions that let illness form.

The bite you can't read

A zombie breach auto-mints a Bite: a bleeding Wound plus a face-down Infection? card whose true value — harmless graze or the turn — stays hidden, auto-flipping at a fixed 60 in-game hours. There is no fight verb and no inspection tool in the slice; the horror is the two-and-a-half days of not knowing while you keep tending the fire.

Loneliness is an appetite with an empty pantry

Solitude decays like thirst but has no true food — only diminishing palliatives (journal, radio static, a keepsake) that lose their taste. Its ceiling drifts down every day (75 − 3·day), so a perfect routine tops out lower each morning. It never kills directly — it makes everything else more lethal, which is the mechanical statement that being alone is a survival threat.

The world withdraws on a clock you can't avert

A deepening Cold Front turns cold into a second hunger; the radio degrades message by message to static then silence on a datable countdown; daylight contracts ~35 minutes a day; fuel dwindles with no resupply; foraged food rots visibly in place. Winter is a week-long boss you can read on the calendar and never stop.

Fighting is the authored failure state

You never have a viable combat build. The single axe panic-swing is the one action with no preview — the fairness contract itself breaks when you're reduced to violence — and a "won" fight reliably spikes Noise + Scent and leaves a Corpse whose Scent floor summons the next approach. Picking up the axe is how the lonely attrition sim tells you it has already gone wrong.

§ 05

Survival Systems

Interlocking parallel needs. survival-needs is the sole authority for all drain rates and the Warmth meter.

Two-layer hunger Satiation → Calories

A fast "hungry feeling" layer over a deep, slow starvation clock — a forgiving grace window that also supplies the Malnourished flag the infection cascade needs.

  • Satiation drains ~−4.5/h awake. Cold food is silent; cooking at a lit hearth adds warmth and morale but mints Scent.
  • Calories (the true starvation clock, lethal at 0) burns only while Satiation = 0, at ~−2.5/h — ~28h of empty-stomach grace.
  • Malnourished = Satiation = 0 OR Calories < 30; printed on the Survivor card because it is load-bearing for wound → infection.

Thirst Hydration

The reliable steady secondary killer — roughly a once-or-twice-a-day drink.

  • Drains ~−5.0/h; Fever multiplies it ×1.5; heavy work adds a flat cost.
  • Boiled Water is safe; Dirty Water hydrates but adds an infection tick. Pipeline: Dirty Water + Pot + lit Hearth → 3 Boiled Water, stored in the Water Store (cap 5).
  • Hot water drunk fresh gives a small one-time Warmth bonus — a quiet warmth source that doesn't broadcast.
  • Staged: Thirsty → Parched → Dehydrated → 90-min terminal countdown.

Body Warmth the slice's spine

A single meter driven by net delta. The world layer only exports one scalar — ambient_cold_pressure — into the cold side of the sum; it never touches the meter directly.

  • warmth_delta/h = Σ heat − Σ cold_pressure. Fire adds +6/+12/+18 by burn level; Wet adds +6 cold; outdoors and night add more.
  • Bands: Warm 100–60 / Cool 59–40 / Cold 39–25 / Freezing 24–10 / Hypothermic 9–1. Terminal at 0 (60-min countdown).
  • Day-6 Deep Freeze: cold so high a steady dry fire barely offsets it — forcing the keepsake-burn.
  • Warmth is the warmth-vs-quiet cascade in one number: the cheapest way to stay warm (a roaring fire) is the loudest, brightest thing you own.

Energy & Sleep

The exhaustion axis feeding the fever cascade; at zero it forces collapse, not death.

  • Drains ~−4.0/h awake; regenerates asleep. Below 20 mints Exhausted (a Fever ingredient).
  • Energy at 0 forces 6–8h sleep wherever you stand — Warmth keeps draining, dangerous outdoors or by a dead fire.
  • Sleeping on a high-Presence night is unsafe, so a loud night can force you to stay awake and dark.

Wounds & Bleeding status-card model

Wounds are status cards with sub-states, not a health-bar dip.

  • A Wound carries {clean/dirty, bleeding?}. Bleeding emits Scent — triage of wounds is triage of Presence.
  • Sources in slice: a chopping mishap (likelier when Exhausted/Freezing) and the zombie breach auto-Bite. No player combat produces wounds.
  • Clean turns Dirty after ~4h exposed; Dirty + Malnourished self-assembles Infection.
  • Bandage → Survivor → Treated Wound: stops bleeding and Scent, halts infection. Clean with Boiled Water first to upgrade Dirty → Clean.

Illness self-assembly single infection counter

Emergent recipes over docked status cards, deliberately simplified: one counter, not a two-stat immune model.

  • Fever: Wet + Cold + Exhausted coexisting 30 continuous minutes (a named anti-flicker buffer) — accelerates thirst, drags morale.
  • Infection: one infection_progress counter rises while Dirty Wound + Malnourished persist, falls once Treated; staged thresholds gate severity.
  • The face-down Infection? from a bite is separate: a fixed 60-hour reveal, deterministic for the slice.
§ 06

The Mind — The Vigil

Solitude, Hope, Morale and The Road: one reconciled system with one owner. It never has its own health bar and never game-overs on its own — Depression kills through the body.

Loneliness is a standing negative that lowers your resilience so every physical insult lands closer to the cliff, and hope is the only forward pull — visible, costly, and the loudest thing you can choose to feel.

  • Solitude decays continuously with no true cure, only diminishing palliatives (journal, radio, keepsake dwell). Its ceiling drifts down each day (75 − 3·day_index) — perfect self-care tops out lower every morning.
  • Morale is derived, not summed: target = 50 + 0.25(Solitude−50) + 0.20(Hope−50) + warmth_bonus + buffers − Σ(status weights), eased asymmetrically (heals slow, sinks fast). Each physical status contributes exactly one weight, counted once. Start 60.
  • One spiral card — Depressed, minted at Morale ≤ 25 held 2h (clears at ≥ 45 held 1h). While docked it stalls the hands (×1.4 action time), accelerates hunger & thirst ×1.3, deepens Solitude, and halves palliative gains. You can't consume your way out — you must fix root causes.
  • Hope never rises on its own (bleeds ~0.4/h, ceiling capped by Solitude+15). Raised only by the vigil lamp, a new radio message, or a keepsake dwell; permanently cut by burning a keepsake.
  • The Vigil lamp (Lamp Fuel + Signal Lamp): +Hope, slows overnight Solitude decay ×0.6, advances The Road one rung next morning — and mints the biggest single Light signal in the game, steering the night's dead toward the one warm window.
  • The Road: a visible, monotonic log card with 5–6 ordered named rungs in the flat unreliable-narrator voice. A lit night advances it; an unlit night slips it back (shown in the dusk projection beside the Presence rise). Day-7 resolution is gated off the rung reached.
  • P(arrival) is stored as a raw float but never rendered numerically — surfaced only as the rung reached plus a qualitative band, so hope reads as fragile, not as an XP bar.
Balance mandateA survivor who never lights the lamp must measurably decline — Solitude and Hope visibly fall and Depressed becomes a real threat within ~2–3 dark nights — so "never light it" is never a dominant strategy.
§ 07

The Card System

No deck, no hand. Cards are persistent objects at absolute positions on one Tableau. You drag one card onto another; on release the game gathers the overlapping group, builds a canonical multiset signature, and lists every matching action with its Time / Yield / Requirement / Presence-Δ before you commit.

That single drag-onto grammar is the entire verb set: craft, eat, drink, treat, feed, boil, light, burn, listen, dowse, and the one blind panic swing. The Survivor is a first-class drop target and the docking parent for all status and signal cards. The honest ledger is ~31 CardData definitions, of which only the 17 authored object cards carry per-card design cost and are governed by the ~16–18 cap.

Crafting model

Recipes are RecipeData resources matched by a multiset signature over the dropped group: consumable inputs matched by id or tag, tools matched as a separate non-consumed predicate, and context matched as predicates (fire:lit, loc:cabin, weather overlays). A prebuilt RecipeIndex gives O(1) lookup. Precedence: id-match over tag-match, fewer leftovers, known over discoverable. Obvious diegetic recipes are always known; the only gated content is the two Found Notes. Illness recipes are the same grammar, evaluated passively against the docked-status set.

§ 08

Threats

The dead are the acute punctuation on a slow sentence of attrition. Almost nothing here is fought.

The Dead zombie-as-weather

Behavior: Never hunt. Each night one Zombie reads effective Presence via one convergence model — pull = Light·1.0 + Heat·0.8 + Noise·1.0 + Scent·0.9 — stepping a distance index across four telegraphed states (Treeline→Yard→Door→Breach) on a 30-minute cadence. Dusk spawn distance is seeded by the day's accumulated Presence. Stiffened under the Cold Front; effectively Frozen on the Day-6 Deep Freeze.

Counterplay: Go dark (dowse hearth/lamp → Presence collapses → it drifts back), hold still / sleep-dark, or the one-decoy bait. Going dark is never the same twice — its cost escalates with the deepening cold.

The Cold Front week-long boss

Behavior: A per-day cold-pressure ramp plus a diurnal swing, exporting one ambient_cold_pressure scalar into the Warmth meter. Turns cold into a second hunger; holds one escalation state — the Day-6 Deep Freeze. A single wet-wood flag on the scripted snow beat makes fresh wood resist catching.

Counterplay: Feed the fire (loud), insulate at the hearth, drink hot water (quiet warmth), dry off Wet, ration daylight for outdoor runs, and — on the climax night — burn a keepsake.

The Bite hidden-timer dread

Behavior: Auto-minted only by a breach at contact: a bleeding Wound plus a face-down Infection? whose true value (graze or the turn) is fixed at spawn, revealing at 60 in-game hours.

Counterplay: Dress the wound to stop the Scent and mundane infection (cannot change a blood-borne turn); otherwise wait and watch. No inspection tool, no amputation in the slice — the horror is the not-knowing.

The Long Decline the true game

Behavior: Hunger, thirst, Warmth, energy and infection drain in parallel with staged effects; neglecting one accelerates others via a modifier matrix. Death is always the first need to reach its terminal countdown, authoring the obituary cause.

Counterplay: Read the pinned danger list, pre-empt cascades by breaking any one ingredient (dry off, eat, dress, sleep), keep the status dock empty.

Loneliness & Depression kills through the body

Behavior: Solitude decays with no true cure under a ceiling that lowers each day; low Solitude drags Morale; Morale ≤ 25 held 2h mints Depressed, which stalls the hands, halves palliatives, and accelerates hunger and thirst.

Counterplay: Light the vigil, dwell on a keepsake, tune the radio, finish real tasks for the accomplishment buffer, and stay warm and fed — root causes, not comforts.

§ 09

World & Time

One location cluster — Cabin hub, Woodline, Creek — across seven in-game days into a deepening cold snap that breaks into a thaw on Day 7.

The world withdraws on a clock you can watch but never avert: the radio dies message by message to static, daylight contracts ~35 min/day, fuel dwindles with no resupply, food rots in place, and the cold ramps to a Day-6 Deep-Freeze climax. Emptiness is authored content — a hard budget of ≤4 unsolicited world lines per day, empty days logged flat ("Day 5. Grey. You boiled water. You waited. No one on the road."), and an indifferent world that never celebrates or reassures the survivor.

Time model

Real-time-with-pause on a single central SimClock — the one authoritative tick. Once per whole game-minute it calls a static Sim.step(1) running an ordered ~11-stage pipeline (environment → modifiers → drains → statuses → emergent recipes → illness → Presence recompute → thresholds → night convergence → scheduled beats → danger-sort/death) purely over GameState. No Timer nodes anywhere. One constant REAL_SECS_PER_GAME_MIN ≈ 0.35 (~8.4 real min per game-day at 1×); a full 7-day run is ~59 min at 1×, ~15–30 min with routine 2×/4× use. Speeds: pause / 1× / 2× / 4×, plus dev skip-to-dusk / skip-to-Day-N. Night is the only convergence window, and travel is a daytime activity, so survivor-at-cabin ≡ cabin-Presence always holds at night.

Weather

Exactly one system: a deepening Cold Front. A per-day cold-pressure ramp (the arrays that are the difficulty curve) plus a cheap diurnal sine, exporting one scalar into Warmth. It holds a single discrete state beyond Normal: the Day-6 Deep-Freeze night, load-bearing because the zombie must be Frozen so the finale is cold + the keepsake-burn, not the dead. Two named rule-patches ship: cold = second hunger and wet-wood-resists-catching. Minor variation is a pure hash of (day, minute, seed) so save/load stays deterministic. Day 7 warms — the thaw beat that gates the resolution.

§ 10

Atmosphere & Presentation

Melancholic, isolated, mundane-routine dread in Project Zomboid's register — flat, clinical, past-tense-adjacent prose with no exclamation and no hero framing. Legibility for the body; mystery for the mind and the dead.

Techniques

  • Flat clinical voice for all copy; status flavor as one dry italic line ("[Cold] — Your hands have stopped hurting. That's not good.").
  • Silence as default: ≤4 unsolicited world lines per day, so any line lands as an intrusion.
  • Empty days logged as content; deaths never editorialize — one true detail ("Day 5, 03:12 — hypothermia. The fire had been out four hours. The lamp was still lit.").
  • The mind is felt, not itemized: Solitude and Morale surface as a single band word; P(arrival) as qualitative bands; the bite stays face-down.
  • Emotional establishment is load-bearing: Maren is a specific mundane person (a name, a mended sweater the wrong size, one dull photograph, a note in her hand) present from the opening tableau.
  • Threat auto-pause defaults off (accessibility toggle) so the flat telegraph lines build dread without yanking the player out every 30 minutes.
  • The radio degrading to static on Day 6 is the fridge-hum-that-stops — the one scheduled sound-death, after which the ambient bed thins to wind only.
  • A persistent obituary journal: loss accumulates across runs; even survival is provisional ("A figure on the road at first light. It's her. You live, for now.").
On "text-forward"Zero illustration or asset pipeline — but text-forward means typography does the work art usually does, not that UI is cheap. Cards are PanelContainer + RichTextLabel with BBCode, an embedded serif for tone and an icon-glyph font for inline symbols; per-kind styling via Theme type variations. Every card body carries name / staged readout / one italic residue line / a footer chip row of time·yield·Presence. The single fiddliest Godot gotcha — BBCode plus both embedded fonts rendering correctly inside a card at 1080p — is proven in the M0 skeleton.
§ 11

The Vertical Slice

Prove ONE loop is fun and moving: tend the fire while the cold, the dark, and your own rising Presence press in — and each dusk decide hearth (live) or lamp (hope), knowing the lamp is the loudest thing you own.

Definition of "done"Validated when a first-time playtester hesitates before lighting the lamp and can narrate their own death cascade card by card afterward. Every feature must sharpen warmth-vs-quiet-vs-hope or make a death more legible, or it is cut.

In scope

  • ~31 CardData defs (17 authored object cards under the ~16–18 cap + 4 signal + 6 emergent status + 1 Zombie + 3 location)
  • 9 drag recipes + 2 passive emergent recipes (Fever, Infection)
  • 3 cascades: warmth↔quiet, wet↔cold↔fever, wound↔infection↔morale
  • Presence as a live pip-strip sum, with per-drag Δ and a tonight's-approach forecast before commit
  • Pinned auto-sorted danger list; pause + 1×/2×/4×; contracting day/night; one Cold Front (cold = second hunger, wet-wood flag, Day-6 Deep Freeze)
  • The Vigil: lamp, Solitude/Hope/derived-Morale/Depressed, both keepsakes burnable, The Road ladder, 7 degrading radio messages
  • Night convergence: one Zombie on the 4-state approach; breach → auto-Bite (Wound + face-down Infection?, 60h reveal)
  • Two timed Travel actions (Woodline 20m, Creek 15m) with Ground abandonment + 6-slot inventory
  • Save/load + a persistent obituary journal across runs
  • Ship-quality tone prose where its system lands (radio, endings, keepsake-burn, dusk telegraph)
  • Observability tooling front-loaded: live inspector, skip controls, set-stat/spawn console, headless batch runner

Out of scope (deferred)

  • Combat depth: weapons table, Fight/Barricade/Bait-full/Flee, Downed states, amputation, the weighted-outcome engine (only the blind axe swing ships)
  • The Prowler / any wild animal, butchering, Meat, claw wounds, the Snare
  • Creek-freeze, Frozen Creek, Snow cards (Creek stable all week; Day-3 beat = wet firewood)
  • Four intermediate weather sub-states, the propagation-multiplier matrix, seeded weather replay
  • Hallucination / false-table cards, proxy-pet comforts, sanity beyond Depressed
  • On-map Turning persistence, corpse→Miasma sub-sim, horde-baiting depth
  • Skill trees / XP, multi-location networks, inventory weight economy
  • Blood integrator stat, two-body Immune math, Nausea/overeating
  • Save-migration logic (version field ships; on mismatch, discard and start fresh)
Definition of fun (unprompted playtester)(1) hesitates at dusk before spending the last fuel, watching The Road as the reason it matters; (2) has an "aha" cascade on their second attempt, pre-empting Wet→Cold→Fever because run one taught them; (3) narrates their own death card by card; (4) feels the ache once — choosing which keepsake to burn, or when the lamp draws the thing that ends them; and (5) says some version of "one more run." If a death feels random, the fix is balance and telegraphing, not more content.
§ 12

Build Roadmap

Risk-first ordering. The Presence hook and the sim spine come before content. Amber nodes are decision gates.

M0

Foundation skeleton + font/BBCode proof

Godot 4.7 project; SimClock / CardDatabase / GameState / SaveManager autoloads; CardData/RecipeData/StatData resources; one Theme + serif + icon font proven rendering BBCode inside a card at 1080p; one themed card visible; live stat-inspector stubbed.

M1

Drag-combine table (overlap-group matcher)

Manual _gui_input dragging, Rect2 overlap gathering the group, multiset-signature RecipeMatcher, context menu with time/yield/requirement. Proves the three-card boil_water path first, exercising the multi-card matcher.

SimClock, needs, danger list, pause/speed Phase-0 go / no-go

Presence core — the hook

Four docked signal cards summing to one pip strip; every recipe's Presence-Δ and tonight's-approach forecast shown before commit; the warmth loop (chop → firewood → feed fire) live. Card template + strip + danger-list structure freeze here.

Contracting day/night; Cold Front ramp (cold = second hunger) + wet-wood flag; ConditionRule engine with condition_timers (Fever 30-min buffer); wounds as status cards; breach/bite face-down Infection? (60h reveal). All 3 cascades traceable in the log.

Signal Lamp + shared-pool Lamp Fuel; derived Morale + Solitude/Hope + Depressed spiral; both keepsakes burnable; The Road ladder; 7 real degrading radio messages. Gate: the dusk fuel-split runs with ship-quality prose, a playtester's hesitation is observed, and a never-light strategy measurably declines.

M6

One Zombie on the 4-state approach reading effective Presence; DayExposure→spawn-distance; go-dark with escalating cold cost; auto-pause toggle (default off). Day-6 climax forces a keepsake-burn; Day-7 resolves to arrival / no-arrival / the-light-drew-the-horde off the visible Road rung. Honestly labeled a small new subsystem, not "wiring," with its own night-balance budget.

Tuning & Telegraphing the real deliverable

~15–30 balance iterations, the majority via the headless batch runner (seeded do-nothing / naive-play policies dumping death_cause/day/minute), reserving full playthroughs for feel. Converge the curve so a quiet/dark night is survivable and the Day-6 burn actually bites.

M7

Save/load hardening, journal, readability pass

Semantic-equality save round-trip (int() coercion, no Resource refs in serialized containers, String keys); persistent obituary journal; version field with discard-on-mismatch; one bounded readability pass at 1080p; per-card residue flavor and prose iteration from playtest reactions.

§ 13

Technical Architecture

Godot 4.7 stable, GDScript-first. Control nodes + custom Resources + one central deterministic tick + JSON save. No C++, shaders, physics, Timer nodes, built-in drag-and-drop, or illustration pipeline.

Data model

Static definitions are custom Resources (.tres), never serialized: CardData, RecipeData, StatData, SignalData, ConditionRule. Recipes reference cards by typed pointer so ids can't be mistyped, and the Inspector is the content editor. The only saved thing is GameState — plain scalars/dicts and an array of CardInstance (a RefCounted view-model with uid, def_id, zone, pos, runtime dict, docked_to). Card nodes are views bound to instances. This static/runtime split is what keeps pause, time-scale, and serialization from tangling.

Canonical ownership (contradictions resolved by the council)survival-needs owns all drain rates and the Warmth meter; mind owns derived Morale and the single Depressed card; the deep-hunger pool is calories; one Presence pip scale from SignalData.tres with co-located thresholds; one convergence model (the 4-state pull engine) called by Sim._tick_night; presence_total and phase are typed transients, never serialized.

Key systems

SimClock + Sim.step

One autoload accumulates real time and calls a single static Sim.step(1) running a fixed ~11-stage ordered pipeline, pure over GameState. Signals emit only for UI, after the step — no sim subsystem connects to a tick.

RecipeMatcher (overlap-group)

On release, gather all rect-intersecting cards into a group (primary target + inputs, cap 3 non-tool), build a canonical multiset signature (consumables by id/tag; tools and context as separate predicates), O(1) index lookup. The 2-argument matcher is deleted so the 2-card assumption can't leak back.

Presence (derived, never stored)

Recomputed each tick from docked SIGNAL CardInstance.runtime.value into a typed transient; anchored signals (Hearth, Lamp) dock only while adjacent. Provides preview_delta so tooltip, pull math, and pip strip share one source of truth.

Threshold + ConditionRule engine

Stat thresholds diff docked status cards each tick (mint/clear). Emergent illness evaluated against the docked-status set, timed by a condition_timers dict (rule_id → elapsed), idempotency derived from "is result already docked?".

Night convergence

GameState.horde = {distance_index, timer, frozen}. Zombie_pull (Scent raised to ~0.9) advances the 4-state machine on a 30-min cadence; weather zombie_speed_mult divides cadence (Stiffened/Frozen). Breach auto-mints the Bite.

SaveManager

Writes only GameState to user://save.json (+ journal.json). int() coercion on integer fields; no Resource refs in serialized containers (active_crafts stores {recipe_id, input_uids, remaining_min}); String keys; semantic-equality round-trip test; version field with discard-on-mismatch.

Godot-specific decisions

  • Manual dragging via Card._gui_input (grab/move/release), not _get_drag_data; Tableau owns all hit-testing and z-order via child order.
  • The Survivor is a first-class drop target pinned to a fixed anchor; on release onto it, RecipeMatcher augments the input set with the Survivor's docked instances so Bandage→Wound and Water→Survivor resolve.
  • One current_action slot (+ one queued) for survivor-occupying actions; active_crafts is the array of ambient/station timers only (boil = short locked setup then an unattended simmer).
  • Travel is a lightweight action type in the foundation (not deferred): applies outdoor cold exposure per tick and discards origin Ground cards on completion.
  • Theme type variations (StatusCard / SignalCard / KeepsakeCard) over per-node overrides; BBCode + embedded fonts proven in M0.
  • Minor weather variation as a pure hash of (day, minute, seed) so no RNG state is stored; ambient temp and phase recomputed on load, never saved.
§ 14

Risks & Mitigations

The eight biggest ways this could fail — each surfaced by an adversarial critic, verified as real, and answered in the design above.

Risk Pure-decline motivation

The 7-day arc only depletes, offering nothing that grows except an invisible deferred coin flip.

→ Concretize arrival into The Road — a visible, monotonic ladder that advances after a lit night and slips after a dark one, gating the Day-7 payoff off the rung reached. ~1 system card, no object-budget.

Risk "Never light it" dominates

The dusk dilemma collapses because the cost is concrete/immediate and the benefit invisible/deferred, and the player has no reason to care about Maren.

→ Front-load Maren as load-bearing content; make go-dark bite via the Solitude→Depressed spiral (balance gate: never-lighting declines in 2–3 nights); show both dusk options felt-vs-felt; keep P(arrival) qualitative.

Risk The Presence hook feels hollow

Consequence-free by day, solved by a rote go-dark at night, switched off on the frozen Day-6 climax.

→ Daytime exposure is a persistent visible forecast; go-dark's escalating cold cost is legible in its preview; Day-6 zombies are deliberately frozen so cold + the keepsake-burn is that night's star; keep "the light drew the horde" as a Day-7 ending.

Risk Repetitive logistics grind

9 recipes learned by Day 1; minute-to-minute dominated by a water/wood grind that crowds out feeling.

→ Batch the dullest pipeline (boil yields 3); start Day 1 with a buffer so scarcity bites from Day 3; cut creek-freeze; make ΔPresence, the dusk decision and the radio live from Night 1; audit the choice-vs-wait ratio at the gates and cut a logistics step if it skews to waiting.

Risk One repeated zombie drains tension

A single telegraphed zombie beaten by dowsing, five nights running.

→ Vary nights with existing levers at zero new content: DayExposure sets spawn distance; go-dark's cost escalates until it stops being a clean answer; the nightly lamp choice is the real read-and-react variable.

Risk Drift into a generic zombie game

Combat is simultaneously cut and fully specified, and the design's heaviest energy is on how to fight.

→ Cut all combat depth; ship a single blind axe swing rebalanced so a won fight summons the next approach — no viable combat build. Breach → auto-Bite as an event, not a fight outcome.

Risk Over-legibility becomes a spreadsheet

Full transparency kills the dread it should serve.

→ Keep the physical fairness contract, but surface the mind as a single band word, default threat auto-pause off, and render P(arrival) as bands only — legibility for the body, mystery for the mind and the dead.

Risk Cross-spec contradictions

Two tick models, four Presence scales, three convergence models, double-owned Morale/Warmth, an unbuildable 2-card matcher, JSON round-trip traps.

→ Name one authoritative owner per subsystem and reconcile the rest: single Sim.step; one pip-scale Presence; one 4-state convergence; survival-needs-owned Warmth; mind-owned Morale; one 0.35 time constant; the overlap-group matcher; hardened JSON rules.

§ 15

Open Questions

What the council could not settle on paper — these are the M6.5 tuning targets and the decisions that need your playtest, not more design.

  1. What Warmth heat-vs-cold coefficients per day put Day 6 on a knife's edge — steady fire ≈ ±0, any lapse negative — without making earlier nights trivially safe? (Primary M6.5 target.)
  2. What Presence pull/threshold values make a lit-vigil night reliably reach the Door and a gone-dark night reliably retreat, while leaving room for the DayExposure spawn-distance variation to matter?
  3. How many Road rungs (5 vs 6) and what regression rule (slip one rung vs. stall) best convey fragile hope without feeling hopeless or like a checklist? Does an unlit night ever feel too punishing?
  4. Is a single infection_progress counter legible enough as an "unfair or clear" read, or does the slice need staged thresholds surfaced before considering the deferred Immune model?
  5. Does the go-dark cold cost escalate smoothly enough across the week to read as "a different decision each night" rather than "fine until suddenly lethal"?
  6. What is the right Day-1 starting buffer (firewood, boiled water, lamp fuel) so the teaching day has slack but scarcity bites by Day 3?
  7. Does the 60-hour bite reveal land as dread or as a forgotten timer — and should the true/false split be fixed-deterministic or seeded per run?
  8. Does the arrival ending read as "quiet and provisional" or accidentally "triumphant" in playtest? If triumphant, which radio/obituary lines need re-voicing?
  9. At what real-minute ratio does the choice-vs-wait audit trip the "cut a logistics step" rule — and which step goes first if it does?
§ 16

Inspirations, Mapped

What we take from each source — and what we deliberately invert or add.

Card Survival: Tropical IslandInteraction DNA
The entire interaction model: cards are the world's objects, dragged together on a table to craft/eat/act, real-time-with-pause, no deck and no hand; Findable/Ground/Inventory rows with abandonment on travel; the pinned auto-sorted danger list; every action showing time/yield/requirement before commit; the "aha cascade" of learning interlocking-need chains. We invert its thirst-dominant tropical curve into a cold-dominant winter one.
Project ZomboidAtmosphere & dread
The mundane-routine dread: flat clinical prose, the indifferent world that never celebrates you, loneliness and depression as real mechanical threats, illness that creeps rather than announces, the withdrawal you watch on a clock (power/radio dying), fair deaths you author and can trace, and the melancholy of logging empty days in a dead landscape.
VigilGrafted — the heart
The beating heart the pure attrition sim lacked: the lamp lit in the window for an absent loved one (Maren), turning hope into a chosen, costly, mechanical Presence spike; keepsakes as fuel with a fading Hope value; the radio that never truly answers as a degrading dusk ritual; Found Notes as the only other voices. We add The Road so hope is visible and climbing, not a hidden coin flip.
DownwindGrafted — the signal economy
Presence as a two-sided signal economy — the seed of baiting the world against itself (the lite one-decoy version ships; full horde-luring deferred) — and weather as a rule-patch on how signals propagate (kept to the single Cold Front). The corpse→Miasma sub-sim, hallucination cards, and multi-species animals are deferred hooks.
§ 17

How This Was Made

This document is the synthesized output of an evaluation council run as a multi-phase agent workflow. Four analysts deconstructed Card Survival and Project Zomboid; three designers each pitched an independent concept from a different angle; a three-judge panel scored them and "The Long Quiet" won (126 pts, vs. 117 "Vigil" and 116 "Downwind"), grafting the best of the runners-up. Seven system designers then fleshed out subsystems; five adversarial critics attacked the result; every finding was independently verified; and a final synthesizer integrated the survivors into the GDD above.
