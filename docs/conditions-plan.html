Dead Air · Design & Phased Plan

The Conditions System

A calm six-slider dashboard on the surface; a small, deterministic, traceable illness simulation hidden underneath — surfaced only when the body would feel it.

How to read this. This is a plan, not implemented code — produced by a design workflow so you can steer the depth before we build. Nothing here is in the game yet. Numbers are provisional. The intent is reasonable, legible depth that makes deaths reconstructable, explicitly not a medical sim; the whole first slice is ~1 resource + ~60 lines.
§ 01

Philosophy — dashboard, not simulation

The visible six sliders (Calories, Hydration, Warmth, Energy, Immune, Mental) are a dashboard, not the simulation. Beneath them sit a small set of hidden float accumulators the player never sees as a gauge and never edits directly. A hidden gauge does exactly two things: it bends the rate of a visible slider, and — when it crosses a breakpoint — it surfaces as a named condition card docked to the Survivor. No hidden variable ever gets its own bar.

Complexity lives under the dashboard so the panel stays calm: a healthy Survivor shows six sliders and zero cards. Medical depth only appears on screen when something is actually wrong — and even the reveal teaches, because a plain-language "tell" always precedes the card.

1 · Deterministic, not rolledA known action that hurts you hurts by a fixed amount every time (honoring the GDD's "no disease is ever rolled"). The only randomness is which forage type you find — never whether eating it sickens you.
2 · Traceability is the contractNothing may incapacitate or kill until it has docked a named card and passed a warned stage. The cause action is stamped onto the condition, so the obituary can name it even after the 6-line day log has scrolled.
3 · Scope is cappedThe prototype target is a deliberately tiny set — gut pathogen, gut illness, fever, plus the free existing band-flags. The wider catalogue is backlog, added one at a time only if it earns its keep in playtest.
§ 02

The reveal ladder

The one-line ruleShow it when the body would feel it, name it when it changes what you can do, and never let it kill you before it's named.

  1. Latent. Inside the ignore band — no UI, no log. The body doesn't notice, so neither do you.

  2. Tell. The first breakpoint fires a single grounded first-person line in the day log ("Your stomach tightens and gurgles.") — no card, no name, no number. The fair warning.

  3. Card. The symptomatic breakpoint docks a named condition card that now actively bends the sim.

  4. Stage. The card advances stages as the gauge climbs (Loose Stool → Dysentery), worsening its multipliers with a qualitative readout — never a raw number.

  5. Crisis. The incapacitation/death breakpoint — never reachable without tier 3+ having been on screen first.

Four mechanisms that make the ladder actually work

  • Asymmetric hysteresis. Each stage has a higher enter and lower exit threshold (surface at 72, resolve at ~58), and the breakpoint must be held ~30 in-game minutes before it fires — so a gauge parked near a breakpoint doesn't flicker enter→exit and spam the log.
  • Trajectory, never hidden. A docked card always shows worsening / holding / healing (a glyph from the gauge's cached delta). Numbers stay hidden; direction never does. This is the whole fairness promise — the difference between "diagnostic" and "feels like a bug."
  • Cause stamp. When a bump crosses a stage, the authoring action + day + minute are stamped onto the condition and rendered on the card ("The Runs — unboiled water, Day 2 09:15"). Survives the day log scrolling; the obituary reads from it.
  • Sticky while active. A docked card stays until its gauge falls back through the exit threshold, then resolves with a line ("The cramps have passed") and re-submerges.
§ 03

Hidden state — the gauges under the dashboard

gut_pathogen incubator

The dose you've swallowed but haven't felt yet — a delayed-onset buffer that converts slowly into gut_bug (and feeds fever) over hours, so you drink at dawn and sicken by dusk.

Driven by fixed doses from drinking unboiled water and eating unidentified forage. Boiling and identifying remove the source entirely.

Surfaces never — it has no card. Pure latent substrate; its only visible consequence is the cards it later spawns.

gut_bug

The gut-illness reservoir under Calories + Hydration. Rises as pathogen converts; decays toward 0 with clean intake, rest, and time.

Surfaces as Loose Stool at mid level, advancing to Dysentery at high sustained level. Its bite is a rising multiplier on the visible Hydration drain — that's the readout.

fever_load

Systemic fever intensity — emergent, not authored. A separate gauge so multiple sources (dysentery, later wound infection) can all feed one fever.

Surfaces as Feverish → High Fever (Delirium). Bite is composed multipliers on Hydration & Energy drain plus a Mental tick.

Shared lethal sink = Hydration no new gauge

The prototype deliberately does not add a hidden blood-volume reservoir. Dysentery, vomiting, and fever all raise Hydration's drain multiplier, so many small problems converge on one legible bar. Death is Hydration bottoming out, gated behind the named Dehydrated countdown. The reservoirs interlock instead of multiplying meters.

cold_exposure backlog

Minutes spent Cold and Wet — the reservoir under Warmth, driving hypothermia and chest illness. Listed so the model is complete; added only if the Warmth slider alone under-teaches cold death.

fatigue_debt backlog

Sleep-debt reservoir under Energy — raises the Energy floor so you can't fully refill, erodes Immune, sags Mental. Backlog until an energy-management loop earns it.

Hard guardrail≤ ~6 hidden gauges for the whole game. No body-part maps, no pharmacokinetics, no rules DSL — conditions stay plain dicts like the action table.

§ 04

The condition catalogue

Queasy → Nausea minor · teacher

Cause rising gut pathogen/bug, or a fresh bite of unidentified forage. Deterministic.

Effects the free early warning — appetite loss, small Mental tick, mild ×1.1 action time. It exists to teach the lesson before it turns serious.

Resolution passes in a few hours if the cause stops; clean water speeds it.

Loose Stool → Dysentery ★ serious → lethal

Cause unboiled dirty water & contaminated food, via pathogen → gut_bug. The required dirty-water example lives here.

Effects a persistent Hydration-drain multiplier + a calorie-absorption penalty; Dysentery heavies it and adds Pain — composing with any fever onto the single Hydration sink toward the Dehydrated countdown. The classic traceable dirty-water death.

Resolution boil water (needs fire = Noise/Light cost), rehydrate, rest; severe stage needs finite antibiotics. Fully preventable by boiling.

Food Poisoning (Vomiting) serious

Cause unidentified forage with a high fixed toxin dose, or spoiled stock. Deterministic spike. The required unknown-forage example.

Effects purges the stomach — you lose most recent Calories/Hydration; the Hydration multiplier spikes for the bout. The danger is fluid loss, not the poison.

Resolution ride it out with boiled water + rest. Prevented by identifying forage first — the skill.

Feverish → High Fever (Delirium) serious → lethal

Cause fever_load fed by dysentery/high pathogen (backlog: wound infection).

Effects composed ×Hydration and ×Energy multipliers + a Mental tick — it accelerates the very dehydration gut illness is already driving. Delirium adds mishap chance.

Resolution rest + fluids + treat the underlying source (fever clears once its ingredient is gone). Fever-reducers only mask the readout.

Dehydrated (Collapse-risk) lethal endpoint

Cause Hydration driven low — by ordinary thirst but accelerated by the composed multipliers from Dysentery, Vomiting, and Fever. This is where most gut/fever chains actually kill.

Effects at ≤25 docks the card ("dizzy when you stand"); at ≤8 starts a named 90-minute Collapse countdown that always shows its trajectory.

Resolution drink boiled water and treat the source — fixing thirst alone won't save you if the source keeps pulling.

Collapsing P1 terminal stub

Cause any visible meter hitting 0 — which today has no consequence at all.

Effects gives Phase 1 real stakes: docks a stub with a countdown and writes one assembled cause-of-death line — "You black out. — dehydration, following The Runs (unboiled water, Day 2 09:15)." Closes the traceability loop end to end.

Later becomes the proper staged, treatable countdown in Phase 3.

Malnourished / Cold band-flags existing · free

Read straight off the sliders (Malnourished at Calories < 30; Cold bands off Warmth). No hidden gauge needed.

Effects force-multiplier ingredients — Malnourished enables Wound→Infection and slows healing; Cold bands add ×1.2/×1.4 Calorie drain and feed fever. They turn many cards above from minor to serious.

Chest Cold · Nutrient Deficiency · Despair · Sprain · Frostbite backlog

Each is a self-contained force-multiplier documented in the full catalogue, added via the same pipeline once its driving gauge exists — one at a time, only if a playtest shows the current set under-teaches. Explicitly deferred so the prototype doesn't balloon.

§ 05

How it interlocks

  • One drain mechanism, composed multiplicatively. Active conditions each contribute to a per-meter multiplier map applied once to the existing drain table. Dysentery ×1.4 · fever ×1.5 compose predictably — replacing flat additive ticks that were trivial near full and stacked illegibly.
  • Reservoirs interlock, they don't fog. Dysentery, fever, and vomiting all drive the same Hydration slider, so many minor problems converge on one legible cause of death instead of spawning independent meters. The core anti-medical-sim move.
  • Delayed onset is the signature. Pathogen incubates over hours before converting, separating cause (dawn's drink) from effect (dusk's Dysentery card) — which is exactly why the cause stamp and a persistent health log are mandatory.
  • Band-flags multiply the cards. Malnourished and Cold bands turn a minor card serious — a cold snap is simultaneously a food crisis, and low Immune makes any infection advance faster.
  • Prevention lives in the fiction, at a collapse-era cost. Boiling water beats Dysentery but needs fire = Noise/Light = detectability. The health system and the stealth system share a wall through the fire.
  • Identification is the real skill vs poisoning. Unidentified forage adds a fixed toxin dose every time; identifying it flips a known/unknown flag that removes the source. You learn the rule, you don't gamble.
  • Treatments target the source, not the slider. Antibiotics knock down the bug, rest lowers gut_bug/fever, rehydration refills Hydration but won't save you if the source still pulls. Fever-reducers and painkillers mask the readout without curing — relief is not cure.
  • Guardrails. Band-tax and condition-tax both scale the same drain, so they compose in one documented ordered pass (no silent double-counting), with a cap on the total stacked multiplier to prevent an unfair 10-second death.
  • Action-driven countdowns. Time only passes on an action, so "do nothing" is a legal stall — the countdown card must make its ticking legible on every action so the timer is felt without a real-time process.
§ 06

Worked examples

The canonical dirty-water death — traceable across a scrolled log
Day 2 08:10 — Drink unboiled: pathogen +dose, latent, no UI. → hours of other actions; the 6-line log has scrolled away. → ~12:40 tell: "Your stomach tightens and gurgles." → 13:20 Loose Stool docks, stamped "unboiled water, Day 2 08:10"; Hydration drain multiplier turns on; you see it falling faster than you drink and the glyph shows worsening. → you keep working → Dysentery. → Hydration ≤25 docks Dehydrated. → still no boil/rest → ≤8 arms the 90-min Collapse → Collapse. Obituary reads from the stamp: "Died of dehydration, following Dysentery — drank unboiled water, Day 2, 08:10."
Deterministic unknown-forage poisoning — no roll, learnable
Day 3 — Forage returns unidentified berries. Eat (unknown): fixed toxin dose + a Queasy warning. You eat two more unknown handfuls — each the same fixed dose, cumulative — crossing the vomit threshold → Food Poisoning: recent Calories/Hydration purged. The lesson is unambiguous: it wasn't unlucky, it was three unknown handfuls. Next run you identify forage first; the same three handfuls are harmless — mastery, not dice.
The near-miss recovery — the system is fair, not a spiral
Day 4 — Loose Stool docks, glyph worsening. This time you read it: add firewood to the hearth (accept the Noise/Light cost), boil the next canister, drink boiled water, rest. The pathogen source is cut; gut_bug decays; the glyph flips to healing; after the hold buffer it resolves: "The cramps have passed." Hydration never reached the Dehydrated band. You paid a detectability cost and a day — but you lived, and you learned.
Two small problems converging on one sink
Dysentery is active (Hydration ×1.4). Later fever_load crosses band and Feverish docks (×1.5 Hydration, ×1.3 Energy). The two multipliers compose on the single Hydration slider, which plummets — neither alone would have killed, but together they reach the countdown. Obituary: "Died of dehydration, following Dysentery and Fever — unboiled water, Day 2." One legible cause, not a fog of meters.
§ 07

UI surfacing

Honest framing: the log/tell surface already exists; the condition-card surface does not and must be built. Today the left panel is a hardcoded "YOU" + a "CONDITION" header on the six bars.

Phase 1 UIA compact chip tray under the existing CONDITION header, rebuilt in the pure-view _refresh() from the conditions list, rendering only conditions whose current stage is not hidden. Each chip: the stage name tinted by severity, a trajectory glyph (worsening/holding/healing), and the cause stamp on hover. Latent gauges draw nothing. A newly docked chip pulses once and drops a log line. Condition ticks fold into advance_time like drain, so they deliberately don't pop a +/− diff label — the erosion should feel like an unexplained slide you must diagnose from the card.
The mandatory second surfaceA persistent health log (separate from the 6-line day ring) that timestamps condition enters/exits + their cause action, so causality survives the day log scrolling. Given delayed onset, this isn't optional — it's the traceability contract made visible.

Phase 3 UI: promote the bespoke portrait into a real Survivor card with a condition CardRow docked to it — matching the GDD's "afflictions are cards on the character," and giving the later detectability trailing-cards somewhere to dock. Never add a slider for a hidden gauge; the card is the only readout.

§ 08

Phased implementation

PHASE 1

Prove the pipeline end-to-end — with stakes

Repeated unboiled drinking surfaces "The Runs", Hydration visibly bleeds via a composed multiplier, a cause-stamped chip with a trajectory glyph appears, and hitting 0 docks a "Collapsing" stub that writes a real cause-of-death line. Scope: a condition_def.gd Resource + one gut_bug.tres (3 stages); in game.gd a conditions map + stage/hold timers, add_condition, _tick_conditions as one line in advance_time, composed multipliers on the drain; the persistent health log; a minimal terminal check + assembled obituary; a cond key in _perform; the chip tray. ~1 resource + ~60 lines.

PHASE 2

Delay + a second path

Consequences stagger across hours and stay traceable: a dawn drink sickens you by dusk; unknown forage deterministically loads toward Food Poisoning. Scope: the hidden gut_pathogen incubator that converts in _tick_conditions; a fever.tres; hook forage with a deterministic fixed toxin dose + a known/unknown flag so identifying removes the source. Randomness only in which forage type spawns.

PHASE 3

Cards, capacity loss, authored death

Conditions become tokens on a real Survivor card; being sick makes you slower; death produces a dated, fully-reconstructed obituary; and there are answers (boil/rest/meds) so it's fair. Scope: Survivor CardIcon + condition CardRow; a stage activity-multiplier scaling action time/yield; upgrade the stub into staged treatable countdowns; add the Boil-water recipe, Rest action, and finite antibiotics — each targeting a source gauge.

PHASE 4

Breadth — only via the same pipeline, only if it earns its keep

One additional condition at a time, each a self-contained ConditionDef + action hook, zero new systems: hypothermia, wound infection, exhaustion, chest cold, and the rest — added only where a playtest shows the current set under-teaches.

§ 09

Open questions — your calls

  1. Two-layer hunger. The GDD defines Satiation (fast) over Calories (slow, lethal reservoir); the prototype flattened it to one Calories meter. Restore the pairing (it's a free worked example of the exact hidden-reservoir motif) or keep it flat — but stop the doc and code disagreeing.
  2. Countdown semantics. Time only passes on an action, so a "90-minute" countdown only advances when you act. Is "do nothing" an intended legal stall, or must an idle player still feel pressure? How is the timer legible each action without a real-time tick?
  3. Multiplier cap. With band-tax and condition-tax both scaling drain, what's the maximum stacked Hydration multiplier before it feels like an unfair instant death? Need a documented cap + compose order.
  4. Hysteresis tuning. Enter/exit gap and hold-buffer (~30m?) that kills flicker without making a genuinely worsening condition feel unresponsive under coarse 5–45m actions.
  5. Meds economy. How many antibiotics/painkillers/fever-reducers on the map? Too few and dysentery is unfair; too many and boiling water (the intended lesson) is bypassed.
  6. Trajectory glyph granularity. Three states (worsening/holding/healing) vs finer — does more precision leak the hidden number and undercut diagnose-it-yourself?
  7. Health-log placement. The 3-column layout has no obvious home for a scrollable health/event log distinct from the day panel — right column, a toggle, or the Survivor card's inspection?
  8. Forage identification UX. How do you identify forage to flip the known/unknown flag — a dedicated action, a skill threshold, a field-guide item? The entire skill against poisoning, currently unspecified.
